## Introduction

Shipt is a same-day delivery platform owned by Target, connecting shoppers with customers who want groceries and household essentials delivered to their door. Operating in partnership with Target, Meijer, H-E-B, and other retailers across 5,000+ cities, Shipt offers a gig-economy model where independent contractor shoppers pick up orders, shop for items, and deliver them within a specified time window.

This guide is based on a comprehensive review of dozens of real employee experiences shared across job review sites, forums, and social media — not a single person's opinion, but a balanced summary of what actual workers report.

If you're weighing Shipt against other delivery gigs, here's the real picture.

## What You'll Actually Do

As a Shipt shopper, your job is essentially personal grocery shopping plus delivery. You use the Shipt Shopper app to browse available orders in your area. Each order shows the store, number of items, delivery window, and estimated pay range before you claim it. Once you accept an order, you drive to the store, shop for every item on the customer's list, bag the groceries, check out using Shipt's payment system, and deliver everything to the customer's door within the specified time window.

Communication with customers is a significant part of the job. Shipt emphasizes a personal, high-touch experience. You're expected to text customers about substitutions, send updates on your shopping progress, and confirm delivery. Many regular Shipt customers develop preferences and will request specific shoppers they've had good experiences with — these "preferred member" relationships can become a reliable income source.

Orders range from small 10-item shops to massive 60+ item orders. Target orders are the most common, but Shipt also services other retailers depending on your market. Some orders are delivery-only (pre-shopped items you just pick up and deliver), while most require full in-store shopping.

Unlike some gig apps, Shipt operates on a delivery window system rather than ASAP delivery. This means you can plan your schedule around specific time slots and manage multiple orders more predictably.

## Pay & Hours

Shipt pay is calculated per order and varies based on the estimated effort: number of items, store, and delivery distance. Typical order pay ranges from $6 to $15 before tips. Tips are not shown upfront — they arrive after delivery, sometimes hours or even days later. This delayed tip visibility is one of the most frustrating aspects for many shoppers.

Most Shipt shoppers report earning $15 to $30 per hour gross before expenses when including tips. After gas, vehicle wear, and self-employment taxes, net earnings typically fall to $10 to $22 per hour. Earnings vary substantially by market, with suburban areas near busy Target locations generally performing best.

Building a base of preferred members who tip well and request you regularly is the key to consistent higher earnings. Experienced shoppers with strong preferred member lists can earn significantly more than newcomers still building their reputation.

There are no minimum hours. You choose which orders to accept and when to work. Peak demand typically falls on weekends, especially Sunday, and around major holidays. Shipt doesn't use a scheduled block system like some competitors — you simply claim available orders as they appear.

## Pros

1. **Personal connection with customers**: Shipt's emphasis on communication and preferred members means you can build genuine relationships with repeat customers. This leads to more consistent tips and a more personalized work experience.

2. **Flexible scheduling without blocks**: Unlike platforms that require you to schedule shifts, Shipt lets you claim individual orders freely. You work when you want without committing to time blocks.

3. **Higher earning potential with experience**: As you build your preferred member list and learn efficient shopping routes, your earning potential increases over time. Experienced shoppers consistently outperform new ones.

4. **Physical activity**: Like Instacart, Shipt keeps you active — walking through stores, carrying groceries, and staying on your feet. It's a welcome alternative to sedentary work.

5. **Target partnership advantages**: The strong partnership with Target means consistent order volume in most markets. Target's clean, well-organized stores also make shopping more efficient compared to some other retail partners.

## Cons

1. **Tips are hidden until after delivery**: Unlike platforms that show tips upfront, Shipt doesn't reveal tip amounts until after you've completed the order. This makes it impossible to evaluate an order's true profitability before accepting it.

2. **Low base pay without tips**: Order pay of $6 to $15 before tips is often insufficient for the time and effort involved, especially for large orders. You're heavily dependent on customer generosity.

3. **Inconsistent order availability**: Order volume fluctuates significantly by market and day. Some markets have more shoppers than orders, creating fierce competition for profitable shops.

4. **Customer support can be unhelpful**: Multiple shoppers report that Shipt's support team is slow to respond and often unable to resolve issues effectively, which is especially frustrating when problems arise mid-order.

5. **Rating system impacts order access**: Customer ratings affect your access to available orders. Low ratings — sometimes given for factors beyond your control like out-of-stock items — can reduce your order options and earning potential.

## Tips for New Employees

1. **Prioritize communication over speed**: Shipt customers value updates and responsiveness. Text when you start shopping, communicate every substitution, and let them know when you're heading to delivery. Great communication leads to better ratings and tips.

2. **Build your preferred member list intentionally**: After each delivery, if the customer tipped well and was pleasant, they may add you as a preferred shopper. Consistently excellent service to these customers creates a reliable income base.

3. **Start with smaller orders**: When you're new and still learning store layouts and the app's workflow, smaller 15-20 item orders are less overwhelming and easier to complete on time.

4. **Learn the Target layout by heart**: Since Target is the primary retail partner, knowing where everything is in your local Target will dramatically increase your shopping speed and hourly earnings.

5. **Track tips by customer**: Keep a simple note of which addresses tip well and which don't. Over time, this helps you make better decisions about which orders to claim, even without upfront tip visibility.

## FAQ

**How is Shipt different from Instacart?**
The core job is similar — grocery shopping and delivery — but Shipt emphasizes a more personal, communication-heavy experience and uses delivery windows rather than ASAP service. Shipt doesn't show tips upfront, while Instacart partially does. Shipt's preferred member system creates stronger shopper-customer relationships. Instacart offers both in-store and full-service roles, while Shipt shoppers always shop and deliver.

**How much do Shipt shoppers make per order?**
Base order pay ranges from $6 to $15 before tips. With tips, most orders yield $12 to $30 total. Earnings per hour (including tips) typically range from $15 to $30 gross, with net earnings of $10 to $22 after expenses. Building a strong preferred member base is key to reaching the higher end.

**Do you need your own bags for Shipt?**
No, you use the store's bags and checkout system. Shipt provides a prepaid card for purchases. However, having insulated bags in your car for temperature-sensitive items is recommended and shows customers you care about quality.

## Conclusion

Shipt stands out among grocery delivery gigs for its emphasis on personal customer relationships and the potential to build a loyal, high-tipping preferred member base over time. It's best suited for people who enjoy interacting with customers, don't mind the uncertainty of hidden tips, and are willing to invest the initial weeks building their reputation. The earning potential grows significantly with experience, making it more rewarding long-term than many other delivery platforms. However, the hidden tip structure and reliance on customer generosity mean your early weeks may feel financially uncertain. If you're patient, communicative, and strategic about building your customer base, Shipt can become one of the more personally satisfying and financially viable gig options available.
