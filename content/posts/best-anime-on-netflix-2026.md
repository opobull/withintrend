Netflix has quietly become one of the best platforms for anime fans. What started as a handful of licensed titles has grown into a massive library of originals, exclusives, and classic series that rivals dedicated anime platforms. In 2026, the streamer is doubling down on anime content with exciting new releases, returning favorites, and an ever-expanding back catalog that covers everything from shonen action to slice-of-life dramas.

Whether you're a lifelong anime fan or someone looking to explore the medium for the first time, Netflix's 2026 anime lineup has something for everyone. Here are the best anime series currently streaming — and the new arrivals you should have on your radar.

## Hottest New Anime on Netflix (2026)

### One Piece — Elbaf Arc

- **Status:** New episodes streaming weekly (April 2026)
- **Genre:** Adventure, action, comedy
- **Episodes (total):** 1,100+

The Straw Hat Pirates are finally sailing to the legendary land of the Giants, Elbaf, and anime fans couldn't be more excited. After the intense Egghead arc, the Elbaf Island Arc promises new adventures, new characters, and the kind of world-building that has made *One Piece* one of the longest-running and most beloved anime series of all time. Netflix has been streaming new episodes as they air, making it easier than ever to keep up with Luffy and the crew.

### Dandelion

- **Release:** April 2026
- **Episodes:** 7
- **Creator:** Hideaki Sorachi (*Gintama*)

Releasing on Netflix in April 2026, *Dandelion* is an adaptation of the one-shot manga by *Gintama* creator Hideaki Sorachi. Netflix is expanding the original 2002 one-shot into a seven-episode anime, and for fans of Sorachi's unique blend of comedy, action, and heart, this is must-watch material. *Dandelion* was Sorachi's first published work — the one that launched his legendary career — making this adaptation a significant moment for anime history.

### JoJo's Bizarre Adventure: Steel Ball Run

- **Status:** Highly anticipated 2026 release
- **Genre:** Action, adventure, supernatural
- **Studio:** David Production

Fans of *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure* have waited four years for the next season, and *Steel Ball Run* is finally arriving. Set in an alternate timeline featuring a cross-country horse race in 1890s America, this part is widely considered by manga readers to be the best JoJo story arc. The combination of Western aesthetics with JoJo's signature style promises something truly unique.

### The Ramparts of Ice

- **Release:** April 2, 2026 (weekly episodes)
- **Genre:** Coming-of-age, romance, comedy

Another April 2026 debut, *The Ramparts of Ice* is a high school romantic comedy following Koyuki's struggles navigating her social life. The series promises comedic moments and emotional depth, adding to Netflix's growing lineup of anime that goes beyond action to explore everyday human experiences.

### Baki-Dou: The Invincible Samurai

- **Genre:** Martial arts, action
- **Studio:** TMS Entertainment

The Hanma saga continues with the latest installment in the *Baki* franchise. *Baki-Dou: The Invincible Samurai* features the legendary swordsman Musashi Miyamoto, who is cloned and brought back to life to challenge the world's strongest fighters. If you're a fan of over-the-top martial arts action and characters with impossibly muscular physiques, *Baki-Dou* delivers exactly what you want — and then some.

## Netflix Original Anime You Can't Miss

### Cyberpunk: Edgerunners

- **IMDb:** 8.3/10 | **RT:** 100% critics
- **Episodes:** 10
- **Studio:** Trigger

Studio Trigger's *Cyberpunk: Edgerunners* remains one of the best anime series Netflix has ever produced. Set in the same universe as the *Cyberpunk 2077* video game, the series follows David Martinez, a street kid who becomes a cyberpunk mercenary in Night City. The animation is breathtaking, the soundtrack is incredible, and the emotional gut punch of the finale will stay with you long after the credits roll. If you only watch one Netflix original anime, make it this one.

### Blue Eye Samurai

- **IMDb:** 8.4/10 | **RT:** 99% critics
- **Episodes:** 8 (Season 1)
- **Status:** Season 2 confirmed

This gorgeous animated series set in Edo-period Japan follows a mixed-race swordsman on a quest for revenge against the few European men who could be his father. *Blue Eye Samurai* is visually stunning, with action sequences that rival anything in live-action cinema. The storytelling is mature and nuanced, dealing with themes of identity, prejudice, and belonging. Season 2 has been highly anticipated, and for good reason — Season 1 was near-perfect.

### Pluto

- **IMDb:** 8.3/10 | **RT:** 100% critics
- **Episodes:** 8
- **Based on:** Naoki Urasawa's manga (reimagining of Tezuka's *Astro Boy*)

Naoki Urasawa's reimagining of Osamu Tezuka's *Astro Boy* arc "The Greatest Robot on Earth" is a masterpiece of sci-fi storytelling. *Pluto* follows Europol robot detective Gesicht as he investigates a series of murders targeting the world's most powerful robots. It's a thoughtful, emotionally rich series that explores what it means to be human through the lens of artificial intelligence.

### Scott Pilgrim Takes Off

- **IMDb:** 7.9/10
- **Episodes:** 8
- **Voice cast:** Original movie cast returns

This anime adaptation of Bryan Lee O'Malley's beloved graphic novels brings back the original movie cast for voice acting while taking the story in completely unexpected directions. If you're a fan of the 2010 film, *Scott Pilgrim Takes Off* is a delightful surprise that both honors and subverts your expectations.

## Essential Classic Anime on Netflix

### Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba

- **IMDb:** 8.6/10 | **MAL:** 8.45
- **Studio:** Ufotable
- **Status:** Multiple seasons + movie available

*Demon Slayer* has become a global phenomenon, and for good reason. The story of Tanjiro Kamado's quest to save his sister and avenge his family is elevated by some of the most stunning animation in anime history, courtesy of Ufotable. If you haven't started this series yet, you're missing out on one of the defining anime of the modern era.

### Vinland Saga

- **IMDb:** 8.8/10 | **MAL:** 8.73
- **Episodes:** 48 across 2 seasons
- **Studio:** MAPPA / WIT Studio

This epic historical anime follows Thorfinn, a young Viking warrior, through a journey that begins as a tale of revenge and evolves into a profound meditation on violence, peace, and the meaning of a true warrior. *Vinland Saga* features complex characters, incredible action, and storytelling that gets better with every arc. Both seasons are streaming on Netflix.

### Jujutsu Kaisen

- **IMDb:** 8.6/10 | **MAL:** 8.61
- **Studio:** MAPPA
- **Status:** Multiple seasons available

The supernatural action series about cursed energy and sorcerers has become one of the biggest anime franchises in the world. *Jujutsu Kaisen* combines explosive fight scenes with dark humor and surprisingly emotional character moments. The animation quality, particularly during battle sequences, is consistently jaw-dropping.

### Attack on Titan

- **IMDb:** 9.1/10 | **MAL:** 8.54
- **Episodes:** 94 (complete series)
- **Studio:** MAPPA / WIT Studio

The epic that redefined anime for a generation is streaming in its entirety on Netflix. Following humanity's desperate fight against the mysterious Titans, *Attack on Titan* starts as a survival horror story and evolves into one of the most ambitious narratives in anime history. All episodes including the final season are available.

### Record of Ragnarok

- **Genre:** Action, mythology, tournament
- **Premise:** Humanity's greatest figures vs. gods from world mythology

This unique premise — humanity's greatest historical figures fighting gods from various mythologies in a tournament to determine humanity's fate — makes for incredibly entertaining anime. From Adam vs. Zeus to Jack the Ripper vs. Hercules, *Record of Ragnarok* offers a cultural mashup of mythological proportions.

## Hidden Gems for Adventurous Viewers

### Dorohedoro

- **IMDb:** 8.1/10 | **MAL:** 8.07
- **Episodes:** 12
- **Studio:** MAPPA

Set in a grimy, magical world where sorcerers experiment on humans, *Dorohedoro* follows Caiman, a man whose head has been transformed into a reptilian shape by a sorcerer's curse. It's bizarre, violent, darkly funny, and utterly unique. The 3D animation style might take some getting used to, but once you're in, you won't want to leave.

### Kotaro Lives Alone

- **Genre:** Comedy, drama, slice-of-life
- **Episodes:** 10

A surprisingly heartwarming series about a four-year-old boy who moves into an apartment complex by himself. What sounds like a bizarre premise becomes a touching exploration of loneliness, resilience, and the unexpected ways people become family. It'll make you laugh and cry in equal measure.

### Great Pretender

- **IMDb:** 7.9/10
- **Episodes:** 23
- **Studio:** WIT Studio

This stylish crime caper anime follows a team of con artists who target the world's worst criminals. With its vibrant color palette, globe-trotting storylines, and jazzy soundtrack, *Great Pretender* feels like an anime *Ocean's Eleven*. It's pure entertainment from start to finish.

### Sirius the Jaeger

- **Note:** Netflix exclusive — leaving September 2026
- **Episodes:** 12

A Netflix exclusive that won't be around forever. Set in 1930s Tokyo, this action series follows a group of vampire hunters. If you're interested, watch soon — it's departing the platform in September 2026.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Netflix good for anime in 2026?

Yes — Netflix has become one of the top platforms for anime, rivaling dedicated services like Crunchyroll. Netflix offers a mix of exclusive originals (*Cyberpunk: Edgerunners*, *Blue Eye Samurai*, *Pluto*), simulcast episodes (*One Piece*), and a deep back catalog of classic series. The main disadvantage vs. Crunchyroll is a smaller overall catalog, but Netflix's curated selection and production quality are excellent.

### Does Netflix simulcast anime (same-day as Japan)?

For select major titles, yes. *One Piece* new episodes are available on Netflix shortly after their Japanese broadcast. However, not all anime on Netflix follows this model — many series arrive as complete seasons after they've finished airing in Japan.

### What's the best anime on Netflix for beginners?

Start with *Demon Slayer* (beautiful animation, straightforward story), *Blue Eye Samurai* (accessible to Western audiences, stunning visuals), or *Cyberpunk: Edgerunners* (especially if you enjoy sci-fi or gaming). These titles have broad appeal beyond the typical anime audience.

### Is all of One Piece on Netflix?

Netflix has a large portion of *One Piece* episodes, and new Elbaf Arc episodes are being added weekly starting April 2026. However, availability of older arcs may vary by region. Check your local Netflix library for the specific episode range available.

### What anime is leaving Netflix in 2026?

Anime titles rotate based on licensing agreements. Notable departures include *Sirius the Jaeger* (leaving September 2026) and *Forest of Piano* (leaving September 2026). Check Netflix's "Last Chance" section or sites like What's on Netflix for up-to-date departure dates.

### Does Netflix have anime movies too?

Yes — beyond series, Netflix streams anime films including *Your Name* (Makoto Shinkai), *Suzume*, Studio Ghibli films (in most regions), and Netflix original anime films. The anime movie selection is smaller than the series library but includes several must-watch titles.
