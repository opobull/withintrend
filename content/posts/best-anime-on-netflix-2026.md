Netflix has quietly become one of the best platforms for anime fans. What started as a handful of licensed titles has grown into a massive library of originals, exclusives, and classic series that rivals dedicated anime platforms. In 2026, the streamer is doubling down on anime content with exciting new releases, returning favorites, and an ever-expanding back catalog that covers everything from shonen action to slice-of-life dramas.

Whether you're a lifelong anime fan or someone looking to explore the medium for the first time, Netflix's 2026 anime lineup has something for everyone. Here are the best anime series currently streaming — and the new arrivals you should have on your radar.

## Hottest New Anime on Netflix (2026)

### One Piece — Elbaf Arc

The Straw Hat Pirates are finally sailing to the legendary land of the Giants, Elbaf, and anime fans couldn't be more excited. After the intense Egghead arc, the Elbaf Island Arc promises new adventures, new characters, and the kind of world-building that has made *One Piece* one of the longest-running and most beloved anime series of all time. Netflix has been streaming new episodes as they air, making it easier than ever to keep up with Luffy and the crew.

### Dandelion

Releasing on Netflix in April 2026, *Dandelion* is an adaptation of the one-shot manga by *Gintama* creator Hideaki Sorachi. Netflix is expanding the original 2002 one-shot into a seven-episode anime, and for fans of Sorachi's unique blend of comedy, action, and heart, this is must-watch material. *Dandelion* was Sorachi's first published work — the one that launched his legendary career — making this adaptation a significant moment for anime history.

### The Ramparts of Ice

Another April 2026 debut, *The Ramparts of Ice* brings a fresh story to Netflix's growing anime portfolio. The series has been generating buzz for its distinctive art style and compelling premise, adding to Netflix's reputation as a platform willing to take risks on original anime properties rather than relying solely on established franchises.

### Baki-Dou: The Invincible Samurai

The Hanma saga continues with the latest installment in the *Baki* franchise. *Baki-Dou: The Invincible Samurai* features the legendary swordsman Musashi Miyamoto, who is cloned and brought back to life to challenge the world's strongest fighters. If you're a fan of over-the-top martial arts action and characters with impossibly muscular physiques, *Baki-Dou* delivers exactly what you want — and then some.

## Netflix Original Anime You Can't Miss

### Cyberpunk: Edgerunners

Studio Trigger's *Cyberpunk: Edgerunners* remains one of the best anime series Netflix has ever produced. Set in the same universe as the *Cyberpunk 2077* video game, the series follows David Martinez, a street kid who becomes a cyberpunk mercenary in Night City. The animation is breathtaking, the soundtrack is incredible, and the emotional gut punch of the finale will stay with you long after the credits roll. If you only watch one Netflix original anime, make it this one.

### Blue Eye Samurai

This gorgeous animated series set in Edo-period Japan follows a mixed-race swordsman on a quest for revenge against the few European men who could be his father. *Blue Eye Samurai* is visually stunning, with action sequences that rival anything in live-action cinema. The storytelling is mature and nuanced, dealing with themes of identity, prejudice, and belonging. Season 2 has been highly anticipated, and for good reason — Season 1 was near-perfect.

### Pluto

Naoki Urasawa's reimagining of Osamu Tezuka's *Astro Boy* arc "The Greatest Robot on Earth" is a masterpiece of sci-fi storytelling. *Pluto* follows Europol robot detective Gesicht as he investigates a series of murders targeting the world's most powerful robots. It's a thoughtful, emotionally rich series that explores what it means to be human through the lens of artificial intelligence. The animation quality is exceptional, and the mystery will keep you hooked.

### Scott Pilgrim Takes Off

This anime adaptation of Bryan Lee O'Malley's beloved graphic novels brings back the original movie cast for voice acting while taking the story in completely unexpected directions. If you're a fan of the 2010 film, *Scott Pilgrim Takes Off* is a delightful surprise that both honors and subverts your expectations. The animation style is vibrant and energetic, perfectly capturing the series' video game aesthetic.

## Essential Classic Anime on Netflix

### Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba

*Demon Slayer* has become a global phenomenon, and for good reason. The story of Tanjiro Kamado's quest to save his sister and avenge his family is elevated by some of the most stunning animation in anime history, courtesy of Ufotable. The Hashira Training Arc and beyond continue to push the series to new heights. If you haven't started this series yet, you're missing out on one of the defining anime of the modern era.

### Vinland Saga

This epic historical anime follows Thorfinn, a young Viking warrior, through a journey that begins as a tale of revenge and evolves into a profound meditation on violence, peace, and the meaning of a true warrior. *Vinland Saga* features complex characters, incredible action, and storytelling that gets better with every arc. Both seasons are streaming on Netflix, and together they form one of the most compelling character arcs in recent anime.

### Jujutsu Kaisen

The supernatural action series about cursed energy and sorcerers has become one of the biggest anime franchises in the world. *Jujutsu Kaisen* combines explosive fight scenes with dark humor and surprisingly emotional character moments. The animation quality, particularly during battle sequences, is consistently jaw-dropping. It's the modern shonen anime that action fans can't afford to miss.

### Attack on Titan

The epic that redefined anime for a generation is streaming in its entirety on Netflix. Following humanity's desperate fight against the mysterious Titans, *Attack on Titan* starts as a survival horror story and evolves into one of the most ambitious and controversial narratives in anime history. Love the ending or hate it, there's no denying the series' impact and the quality of its storytelling.

### Record of Ragnarok

This unique premise — humanity's greatest historical figures fighting gods from various mythologies in a tournament to determine humanity's fate — makes for incredibly entertaining anime. From Adam vs. Zeus to Jack the Ripper vs. Hercules, *Record of Ragnarok* offers a cultural mashup of mythological proportions. The character designs are stunning, and the fights are creative and emotionally charged.

## Hidden Gems for Adventurous Viewers

### Dorohedoro

Set in a grimy, magical world where sorcerers experiment on humans, *Dorohedoro* follows Caiman, a man whose head has been transformed into a reptilian shape by a sorcerer's curse. It's bizarre, violent, darkly funny, and utterly unique. The 3D animation style might take some getting used to, but once you're in, you won't want to leave this weird and wonderful world.

### Kotaro Lives Alone

A surprisingly heartwarming series about a four-year-old boy who moves into an apartment complex by himself. What sounds like a bizarre premise becomes a touching exploration of loneliness, resilience, and the unexpected ways people become family. It'll make you laugh and cry in equal measure.

### Great Pretender

This stylish crime caper anime follows a team of con artists who target the world's worst criminals. With its vibrant color palette, globe-trotting storylines, and jazzy soundtrack, *Great Pretender* feels like an anime *Ocean's Eleven*. It's pure entertainment from start to finish.

## Getting Started: Anime Recommendations by Taste

- **New to anime?** Start with *Demon Slayer* or *Blue Eye Samurai* — both are accessible and visually stunning
- **Love action?** *Jujutsu Kaisen*, *Baki-Dou*, or *Vinland Saga*
- **Prefer sci-fi?** *Cyberpunk: Edgerunners* or *Pluto*
- **Want something unique?** *Dorohedoro* or *Scott Pilgrim Takes Off*
- **Looking for emotional depth?** *Kotaro Lives Alone* or *Vinland Saga*
- **Following the biggest franchises?** *One Piece* (Elbaf Arc) or *Attack on Titan*

Netflix's anime library in 2026 is deeper and more diverse than ever. The platform's commitment to both licensing established hits and producing original anime content means there's always something new to discover. Whether you're a veteran weeb or an anime-curious newcomer, there's never been a better time to explore what the medium has to offer.
