Whether you're cuddled up with a partner, having a solo movie night, or hosting a girls' night in, there's nothing quite like a good romance movie. Netflix's 2026 romance catalog is impressively diverse, ranging from swoon-worthy adaptations of bestselling novels to quirky indie rom-coms and sweeping period dramas. Whatever kind of love story you're in the mood for, Netflix has you covered.

We've sorted through the platform's current offerings to bring you the best romance movies streaming right now — organized by mood so you can find exactly what you're looking for.

## New Romance Movies on Netflix (2026)

### People We Meet on Vacation

The highly anticipated adaptation of Emily Henry's bestselling novel arrived on Netflix in early 2026 and immediately became one of the most-watched films on the platform. The story follows two best friends — Alex and Poppy — who take a vacation together every summer until a falling-out two years ago. When Poppy convinces Alex to give their tradition one more chance, old feelings resurface. It's the kind of warm, witty, will-they-won't-they romance that's perfect for a cozy night in. Emily Henry's novels have been dominating the romance genre, and this adaptation does the source material justice.

### Sliding Doors (Newly Added)

This classic 1998 Gwyneth Paltrow film has found a new audience on Netflix in 2026. The movie explores two parallel timelines based on whether the protagonist catches a train or misses it, and how that single moment changes the trajectory of her love life entirely. It's a clever concept that remains fresh and engaging nearly three decades later, proving that great storytelling is timeless.

## Best Romantic Comedies

### Always Be My Maybe

Randall Park and Ali Wong deliver pitch-perfect performances as childhood friends who reconnect after years apart. The chemistry between the leads is undeniable, and the script is genuinely funny from start to finish. But let's be honest — the real reason this movie became a sensation is Keanu Reeves' absolutely legendary cameo as a hilariously exaggerated version of himself. It's one of the funniest scenes in any Netflix movie, period.

### Set It Up

Two overworked assistants (Zoey Deutch and Glen Powell) hatch a scheme to set up their nightmare bosses with each other, hoping it'll make their own lives easier. What starts as a professional survival strategy naturally evolves into something more personal between the two schemers. It's a classic rom-com setup executed with charm and charisma, and it's widely considered one of the best original romantic comedies Netflix has produced.

### To All the Boys I've Loved Before

The movie that launched a franchise and made Noah Centineo a teen heartthrob. Lana Condor is absolutely delightful as Lara Jean, a girl whose secret love letters are accidentally sent to all her former crushes. It's sweet, fun, and captures the butterflies of young love with remarkable sincerity. All three films are streaming, though most fans agree the first one is the best.

### The Kissing Booth

This teen rom-com became a surprise Netflix hit and spawned two sequels. It follows Elle, a high school student who falls for her best friend's older brother — breaking the number one rule of their friendship pact. Is it cheesy? Absolutely. Is it fun? Also absolutely. Sometimes you just want something light and easy, and *The Kissing Booth* delivers exactly that.

### Look Both Ways

Lili Reinhart plays a young woman whose life splits into two parallel timelines on the night of her college graduation — one where she's pregnant and one where she's not. Both paths lead to unexpected romances and personal growth. It's a thoughtful take on the rom-com formula that asks whether there's really such a thing as a "right" path in life and love.

## Sweeping Love Stories

### The Notebook

The quintessential tearjerker romance, adapted from Nicholas Sparks' beloved novel. Ryan Gosling and Rachel McAdams deliver iconic performances as young lovers separated by class and circumstance. You know the rain scene. You know the ending. It still gets you every single time. This is the movie you put on when you want to feel everything.

### Atonement

James McAvoy and Keira Knightley star in this devastating wartime romance about a love destroyed by a child's false accusation. Joe Wright's direction is stunning — the Dunkirk beach sequence alone is one of the most remarkable technical achievements in modern cinema. Be warned: this is not a feel-good movie. It's a beautiful, heartbreaking meditation on love, guilt, and the stories we tell ourselves.

### Pride and Prejudice (2005)

Keira Knightley and Matthew Macfadyen bring Jane Austen's timeless romance to life with crackling chemistry and sumptuous period detail. The hand-flex scene has spawned a thousand memes for good reason — it captures romantic tension more effectively than any kiss in movie history. If you've never experienced this adaptation, you're missing one of the best romance films of the 21st century.

## Indie and Offbeat Romance

### Someone Great

This breakup movie is really a love letter to female friendship. Gina Rodriguez plays a music journalist processing the end of a nine-year relationship by having one last legendary night out in New York with her two best friends. It's funny, messy, and emotionally honest in a way that feels refreshingly real. The soundtrack is also excellent.

### The Half of It

A shy, bookish student is paid to write love letters on behalf of a jock to the girl they both secretly like. This quiet, smart film explores the complexities of love, identity, and connection with warmth and wit. It's a coming-of-age romance that subverts expectations at every turn and features one of the most authentic portrayals of queer teen experience on Netflix.

### Your Name (Kimi no Na wa)

Makoto Shinkai's animated masterpiece is a breathtaking romance about two teenagers who mysteriously begin swapping bodies. What starts as a comedic premise evolves into an epic, emotionally overwhelming love story that spans time and space. The animation is absolutely gorgeous, and the emotional climax will leave you in tears. Even if you don't typically watch anime, this film transcends genre boundaries.

## Feel-Good Romance for Comfort Viewing

### The Proposal

Sandra Bullock and Ryan Reynolds have incredible chemistry in this workplace rom-com about a demanding boss who forces her assistant to pretend to be her fiancé to avoid deportation. It follows every romantic comedy formula beat for beat, and that's exactly why it works. Betty White steals every scene she's in. It's pure comfort food cinema.

### 50 First Dates

Adam Sandler and Drew Barrymore star in this uniquely sweet comedy about a man who falls in love with a woman who has short-term memory loss. Every day, he has to make her fall in love with him all over again. It's silly and sentimental in the best Sandler way, and Barrymore brings genuine warmth to a premise that could easily have been tasteless in lesser hands.

### Love Actually

The ultimate ensemble romantic comedy. *Love Actually* weaves together multiple love stories set in London during the Christmas season. Some plots work better than others (the Colin-goes-to-America subplot is... something), but the highs are genuinely wonderful. Hugh Grant dancing through 10 Downing Street, the airport arrivals montage, the cue card scene — it's become a comfort movie for millions of people worldwide.

## Your Romance Movie Night Cheat Sheet

- **First date movie?** *Always Be My Maybe* or *Set It Up* — funny and low-pressure
- **Need a good cry?** *The Notebook* or *Atonement* — bring tissues
- **Want something smart?** *The Half of It* or *Sliding Doors*
- **Cozy comfort watch?** *The Proposal* or *Love Actually*
- **Something visually stunning?** *Your Name* or *Pride and Prejudice*
- **Based on a bestseller?** *People We Meet on Vacation* — the hottest romance movie on Netflix right now

Netflix continues to be one of the best platforms for romance fans, with new additions arriving monthly and a deep library of classics always available. Whether you're in the mood for butterflies, tears, or both, there's a perfect romance movie waiting for you.
