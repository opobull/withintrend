Whether you're cuddled up with a partner, having a solo movie night, or hosting a girls' night in, there's nothing quite like a good romance movie. Netflix's 2026 romance catalog is impressively diverse, ranging from swoon-worthy adaptations of bestselling novels to quirky indie rom-coms and sweeping period dramas. Whatever kind of love story you're in the mood for, Netflix has you covered.

We've sorted through the platform's current offerings to bring you the best romance movies streaming right now — organized by mood so you can find exactly what you're looking for.

## New Romance Movies on Netflix (2026)

### People We Meet on Vacation

- **Based on:** Emily Henry's bestselling novel
- **Genre:** Romantic comedy
- **Status:** One of Netflix's most-watched films of early 2026

The highly anticipated adaptation of Emily Henry's bestselling novel arrived on Netflix in early 2026 and immediately became one of the most-watched films on the platform. The story follows two best friends — Alex and Poppy — who take a vacation together every summer until a falling-out two years ago. When Poppy convinces Alex to give their tradition one more chance, old feelings resurface. It's the kind of warm, witty, will-they-won't-they romance that's perfect for a cozy night in.

**If you liked this:** Check out other Emily Henry adaptations — *Beach Read* and *Book Lovers* are also being developed for screen.

### Sliding Doors (Newly Added)

- **Stars:** Gwyneth Paltrow
- **Year:** 1998 (classic, newly added to Netflix)
- **Concept:** Two parallel timelines based on catching/missing a train

This classic 1998 film has found a new audience on Netflix in 2026. The movie explores two parallel timelines based on whether the protagonist catches a train or misses it, and how that single moment changes the trajectory of her love life entirely. It's a clever concept that remains fresh and engaging nearly three decades later.

## Best Romantic Comedies

### Always Be My Maybe

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 90% critics / 73% audience
- **Stars:** Randall Park, Ali Wong
- **The Keanu factor:** Features the greatest celebrity cameo in Netflix history

Randall Park and Ali Wong deliver pitch-perfect performances as childhood friends who reconnect after years apart. The chemistry between the leads is undeniable, and the script is genuinely funny. But let's be honest — the real reason this movie became a sensation is Keanu Reeves' absolutely legendary cameo as a hilariously exaggerated version of himself.

### Set It Up

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 92% critics / 73% audience
- **Stars:** Zoey Deutch, Glen Powell
- **Best for:** Classic rom-com fans

Two overworked assistants hatch a scheme to set up their nightmare bosses with each other. What starts as a professional survival strategy naturally evolves into something more personal. It's widely considered one of the best original romantic comedies Netflix has produced.

### To All the Boys I've Loved Before

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 96% critics / 80% audience
- **Stars:** Lana Condor, Noah Centineo
- **Sequels:** All three films streaming

The movie that launched a franchise and made Noah Centineo a teen heartthrob. Lana Condor is absolutely delightful as Lara Jean, a girl whose secret love letters are accidentally sent to all her former crushes. All three films are streaming, though most fans agree the first one is the best.

### The Kissing Booth

- **Stars:** Joey King, Jacob Elordi
- **Sequels:** Full trilogy available
- **Vibe:** Fun, cheesy teen romance

This teen rom-com became a surprise Netflix hit and spawned two sequels. It follows Elle, a high school student who falls for her best friend's older brother. Is it cheesy? Absolutely. Is it fun? Also absolutely. Sometimes you just want something light and easy.

### Look Both Ways

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 68% critics / 67% audience
- **Stars:** Lili Reinhart
- **Concept:** Two parallel timelines from one night

Lili Reinhart plays a young woman whose life splits into two parallel timelines on the night of her college graduation — one where she's pregnant and one where she's not. Both paths lead to unexpected romances and personal growth. A thoughtful take on the rom-com formula.

## Sweeping Love Stories

### The Notebook

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 53% critics / 85% audience
- **Stars:** Ryan Gosling, Rachel McAdams
- **Based on:** Nicholas Sparks' novel
- **Status:** The ultimate tearjerker

The quintessential tearjerker romance. You know the rain scene. You know the ending. It still gets you every single time. This is the movie you put on when you want to feel everything.

### Atonement

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 83% critics / 78% audience
- **Stars:** James McAvoy, Keira Knightley
- **Awards:** Academy Award for Best Original Score
- **Warning:** Not a feel-good movie

James McAvoy and Keira Knightley star in this devastating wartime romance about a love destroyed by a child's false accusation. Joe Wright's direction is stunning — the Dunkirk beach sequence alone is one of the most remarkable technical achievements in modern cinema.

### Pride and Prejudice (2005)

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 87% critics / 86% audience
- **Stars:** Keira Knightley, Matthew Macfadyen
- **Director:** Joe Wright
- **The moment:** The hand-flex scene

Keira Knightley and Matthew Macfadyen bring Jane Austen's timeless romance to life with crackling chemistry and sumptuous period detail. The hand-flex scene has spawned a thousand memes for good reason — it captures romantic tension more effectively than any kiss in movie history.

## Indie and Offbeat Romance

### Someone Great

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 83% critics / 78% audience
- **Stars:** Gina Rodriguez
- **Really about:** Female friendship

This breakup movie is really a love letter to female friendship. Gina Rodriguez plays a music journalist processing the end of a nine-year relationship by having one last legendary night out in New York with her two best friends. The soundtrack is also excellent.

### The Half of It

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 97% critics / 71% audience
- **Director:** Alice Wu
- **Themes:** Love, identity, queer teen experience

A shy, bookish student is paid to write love letters on behalf of a jock to the girl they both secretly like. This quiet, smart film explores the complexities of love, identity, and connection with warmth and wit. It features one of the most authentic portrayals of queer teen experience on Netflix.

### Your Name (Kimi no Na wa)

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 98% critics / 95% audience
- **IMDb:** 8.4/10
- **Director:** Makoto Shinkai
- **Type:** Animated (anime)

Makoto Shinkai's animated masterpiece is a breathtaking romance about two teenagers who mysteriously begin swapping bodies. What starts as a comedic premise evolves into an epic, emotionally overwhelming love story that spans time and space. The animation is absolutely gorgeous. Even if you don't typically watch anime, this film transcends genre boundaries.

## Feel-Good Romance for Comfort Viewing

### The Proposal

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 44% critics / 69% audience
- **Stars:** Sandra Bullock, Ryan Reynolds, Betty White
- **Vibe:** Pure comfort food cinema

Sandra Bullock and Ryan Reynolds have incredible chemistry in this workplace rom-com about a demanding boss who forces her assistant to pretend to be her fiancé to avoid deportation. Betty White steals every scene she's in.

### 50 First Dates

- **Stars:** Adam Sandler, Drew Barrymore
- **Concept:** He makes her fall in love every day (short-term memory loss)

Adam Sandler and Drew Barrymore star in this uniquely sweet comedy about a man who falls in love with a woman who has short-term memory loss. It's silly and sentimental in the best Sandler way.

### Love Actually

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 64% critics / 72% audience
- **Stars:** Hugh Grant, Alan Rickman, Emma Thompson, Colin Firth
- **Setting:** London at Christmas
- **Status:** The ultimate ensemble romance

The ultimate ensemble romantic comedy. *Love Actually* weaves together multiple love stories set in London during the Christmas season. Some plots work better than others, but the highs are genuinely wonderful. It's become a comfort movie for millions worldwide.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most popular romance movie on Netflix in 2026?

*People We Meet on Vacation* — the adaptation of Emily Henry's bestselling novel — has been one of Netflix's most-watched romance films since its release in early 2026. Among evergreen titles, *The Notebook* and *To All the Boys I've Loved Before* remain consistently popular.

### Does Netflix have good romantic comedies?

Yes — Netflix has one of the strongest rom-com libraries among streaming platforms. Key titles include *Set It Up*, *Always Be My Maybe*, *To All the Boys I've Loved Before*, *Look Both Ways*, and *The Kissing Booth* trilogy. Netflix also regularly produces new original rom-coms.

### What's a good romance movie for couples to watch together?

For a date night, *Always Be My Maybe* or *Set It Up* are ideal — they're funny, feel-good, and not too intense. *Pride and Prejudice* (2005) is perfect for a more sophisticated evening. Avoid *Atonement* if you're looking for a happy ending.

### Are there any Korean romance movies on Netflix?

Netflix has a growing collection of Korean romance content, including K-dramas and films. While the specific titles rotate, Netflix has been investing heavily in Korean content since the success of *Squid Game*. Check the "Korean Movies" or "K-Dramas" categories for current availability.

### What romance movies are coming to Netflix in 2026?

Netflix regularly adds new romance films throughout the year. Additional Emily Henry adaptations are in development, and Netflix typically releases several original rom-coms each quarter. Follow Netflix's Tudum blog for upcoming release announcements.
