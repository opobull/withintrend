Netflix remains the undisputed king of crime television in 2026. Whether you're into gritty detective dramas, nail-biting psychological thrillers, or true crime docuseries that keep you up at night, the platform's library has never been stronger. With new originals dropping regularly and an impressive back catalog of critically acclaimed series, finding your next binge-worthy crime show has never been easier — or harder, given the sheer volume of options.

We've curated this list of the best crime shows currently streaming on Netflix in 2026, considering critical reception, audience ratings, and pure binge-watchability. From fresh releases to timeless classics that still hold up, here are the series that deserve a spot on your watchlist.

## Best New Crime Series on Netflix (2026)

### City of Shadows

One of the most talked-about crime series to land on Netflix recently, *City of Shadows* delivers a taut six-episode thriller that pairs a determined deputy with a shadowy investigation full of psychological depth and suspense. The series masterfully builds tension across its compact runtime, making it perfect for a single-sitting binge. The cinematography is stunning, with moody lighting and tight camera work that puts you right in the middle of every tense confrontation.

### Black Doves

This espionage-crime hybrid series starring Keira Knightley has become a massive hit. Set in a world of political intrigue and criminal underworlds, *Black Doves* follows a spy whose secret life is threatened when her lover is murdered. The show blends crime investigation with high-stakes espionage, offering a fresh take on the genre that keeps you guessing until the very end. Season 2 is already confirmed, which speaks to its massive popularity.

### Missing You

Based on Harlan Coben's bestselling novel, *Missing You* is a gripping limited series about a detective who discovers her long-lost fiancé on a dating app — eleven years after he vanished without explanation. What unfolds is a twisting mystery that connects to unsolved crimes spanning over a decade. The performances are exceptional, and the show does a brilliant job of peeling back layers of deception episode by episode.

### The Gilgo Beach Murders

This true crime docuseries dives into one of America's most chilling unsolved cases. The three-episode series features the infamous 911 calls, exposes secrets of corrupt law enforcement, and traces the clues that eventually led to the arrest of Rex Andrew Heuermann nearly 15 years after the murders. It's a haunting, meticulously researched series that true crime fans will find absolutely riveting.

### The Tylenol Murders

Another standout true crime entry, this three-episode series revisits the 1982 case where seven people died after consuming Extra-Strength Tylenol capsules laced with potassium cyanide. The perpetrator was never found, and the motive remains unknown. The series highlights the prime suspects and explores how this case fundamentally changed product safety standards in America.

## Classic Crime Shows Still Worth Watching

### Breaking Bad

No list of crime shows is complete without *Breaking Bad*. Walter White's transformation from mild-mannered chemistry teacher to ruthless drug kingpin remains one of television's greatest character arcs. All five seasons are streaming on Netflix, and the show's 9.5 IMDb rating speaks for itself. If you somehow haven't seen it yet, 2026 is as good a time as any to experience this masterpiece.

### Ozark

Jason Bateman stars as a financial planner who relocates his family to the Ozarks after a money-laundering scheme goes wrong. *Ozark* is dark, intense, and features some of the best performances in recent television history — Laura Linney and Julia Garner are particularly outstanding. All four seasons provide a complete, satisfying story arc that gets progressively more intense.

### Mindhunter

David Fincher's exploration of the early days of criminal profiling at the FBI is a must-watch for anyone interested in the psychology behind crime. Based on the true story of the FBI's Behavioral Science Unit, *Mindhunter* follows agents who interview imprisoned serial killers to understand how they think. The show is methodical, cerebral, and deeply unsettling in the best way possible.

### Narcos and Narcos: Mexico

The *Narcos* franchise offers an epic, sprawling look at the rise and fall of drug cartels across Latin America. The original series chronicles Pablo Escobar's reign in Colombia, while *Narcos: Mexico* shifts focus to the birth of the Mexican drug war. Together, they form one of the most comprehensive and entertaining crime sagas on television.

### Dark

While technically a sci-fi thriller, *Dark* incorporates crime elements that make it unmissable. This German-language series begins with the disappearance of children in a small town and evolves into a mind-bending mystery involving time travel and interconnected families. It's one of the most intelligent shows Netflix has ever produced, and the crime elements provide a compelling entry point into its complex narrative.

## True Crime Docuseries Worth Your Time

### Making a Murderer

The docuseries that arguably launched the true crime streaming phenomenon, *Making a Murderer* follows Steven Avery and Brendan Dassey through their controversial murder convictions. The series raises profound questions about the American justice system and remains compelling viewing years after its initial release.

### The Innocent Man

Based on John Grisham's only non-fiction book, this docuseries examines two murders in the small town of Ada, Oklahoma, and the questionable convictions that followed. It's a sobering look at how small-town justice can go terribly wrong, with implications that extend far beyond the cases themselves.

### American Nightmare

This limited docuseries tells the harrowing true story of a couple whose kidnapping report was dismissed by police, who then charged the victims with staging the crime. It's a jaw-dropping examination of how law enforcement bias can compound tragedy, and it's one of the most infuriating and compelling true crime stories you'll ever watch.

## International Crime Shows You Shouldn't Miss

### How to Get to Heaven from Belfast

This critically acclaimed series has been gaining attention for its unflinching look at crime in Northern Ireland. The show weaves together multiple storylines against the backdrop of Belfast's complex history, delivering a crime drama that's as much about place and identity as it is about the cases themselves. The performances are raw and powerful, making it a standout among Netflix's international offerings.

### Who Is Erin Carter?

Set in the sun-drenched south of Spain, this series follows a British woman whose seemingly perfect life unravels when a violent supermarket robbery forces her to reveal skills that suggest a hidden past. It's a fast-paced, twisty thriller that keeps you guessing about the protagonist's true identity. The Spanish and British settings give it a unique visual identity that sets it apart from typical crime fare.

## How to Choose Your Next Crime Binge

With so many excellent options, choosing what to watch next can feel overwhelming. Here's a quick guide:

- **Want something short and intense?** Go with *City of Shadows* (6 episodes) or *Missing You* (limited series)
- **Want a long-haul binge?** *Breaking Bad* (62 episodes) or *Ozark* (44 episodes) will keep you busy for weeks
- **Prefer true crime?** *The Gilgo Beach Murders* or *American Nightmare* offer real-world thrills
- **Looking for something international?** *Dark* or *How to Get to Heaven from Belfast* deliver fresh perspectives
- **Want psychological depth?** *Mindhunter* is the clear winner

Netflix continues to invest heavily in crime content, with new series and docuseries arriving monthly. The platform's algorithm also does a solid job of recommending crime content based on your viewing history, so once you start watching, you'll have no shortage of suggestions.

Whatever your taste in crime entertainment, 2026 is shaping up to be an exceptional year for the genre on Netflix. The combination of new originals, international acquisitions, and an unmatched back catalog means there's always something new to discover. Happy binge-watching — just maybe leave a light on.
