Sometimes you just need a good laugh. Whether you've had a long day at work, you're hanging out with friends, or you just want something light and entertaining, a great comedy movie hits different. Netflix's comedy library in 2026 is stacked with options — from sharp-witted originals to beloved classics that never get old.

We've combed through Netflix's current catalog to bring you the best comedy movies streaming right now. This list covers everything from slapstick and rom-coms to dark comedies and action-comedies, so no matter what flavor of funny you're craving, we've got you covered.

## Best New Comedy Movies on Netflix (2026)

### Nobody 2

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 76% critics / 88% audience
- **Runtime:** 1h 42min
- **Genre:** Action-comedy

The sequel to the surprise hit action-comedy is now streaming on Netflix, and it delivers everything fans loved about the original — and then some. Bob Odenkirk returns as the unassuming family man with a very particular set of skills, and the action sequences are even more creative and brutal this time around. If you loved the first one, this is a no-brainer.

### Jeff Ross: Take a Banana for the Ride

- **Type:** Stand-up special
- **Runtime:** ~1 hour

The Roastmaster General himself delivers a hilarious new stand-up special that landed on Netflix in March 2026. Jeff Ross is at his sharpest here, roasting everyone from politicians to audience members with the kind of fearless comedy that's made him a legend. It's the perfect thing to throw on when you want quick, punchy laughs without committing to a full movie.

### Blue Moon

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 89% critics / 76% audience
- **Genre:** Comedy-drama

This charming comedy arrived on Netflix in late 2025 and has been steadily climbing the most-watched lists ever since. *Blue Moon* offers a witty, character-driven story that balances humor with genuine heart. It's the kind of movie that surprises you — you go in expecting a light comedy and come out genuinely moved.

## Classic Comedies That Never Get Old

### The Grand Budapest Hotel

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 92% critics / 87% audience
- **IMDb:** 8.1/10
- **Director:** Wes Anderson

Wes Anderson's masterpiece is a visual feast wrapped in a hilarious caper. Ralph Fiennes delivers a career-best performance as the legendary concierge Gustave H., and the ensemble cast — including Tony Revolori, Saoirse Ronan, Jeff Goldblum, Willem Dafoe, and Edward Norton — is absolutely stacked. Every frame is a painting, every line is quotable, and it somehow manages to be both laugh-out-loud funny and deeply melancholic.

### Superbad

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 88% critics / 86% audience
- **IMDb:** 7.6/10
- **Stars:** Jonah Hill, Michael Cera, Christopher Mintz-Plasse

The 2007 teen comedy that defined a generation is still just as funny nearly two decades later. Jonah Hill and Michael Cera are perfectly cast as two high school seniors trying to buy alcohol for a party before graduation. The McLovin subplot alone is worth the rewatch. If you haven't seen it since it came out, revisit it — the jokes hold up remarkably well.

### The Nice Guys

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 92% critics / 79% audience
- **IMDb:** 7.3/10
- **Stars:** Ryan Gosling, Russell Crowe

Shane Black's criminally underrated buddy comedy set in 1970s Los Angeles pairs Ryan Gosling and Russell Crowe as a private eye and an enforcer investigating a missing girl. Gosling's physical comedy is absolutely brilliant, and his chemistry with Crowe is electric. This movie deserved to be a bigger hit, and its cult following continues to grow on Netflix.

### Knives Out

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 97% critics / 92% audience
- **IMDb:** 7.9/10
- **Stars:** Daniel Craig, Ana de Armas, Chris Evans

Rian Johnson's whodunit is as funny as it is clever. Daniel Craig's Benoit Blanc is an instant-classic detective character, and the ensemble cast makes every scene a delight. The mystery is satisfying, but it's the sharp humor and social commentary that elevate it above standard genre fare.

## Best Rom-Coms on Netflix

### Set It Up

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 92% critics / 73% audience
- **Stars:** Zoey Deutch, Glen Powell

This 2018 Netflix original is a modern rom-com classic. Two overworked assistants decide to set up their demanding bosses with each other, only to develop feelings for each other in the process. It's charming, funny, and features genuinely great chemistry between its leads. If you love the genre, this is essential viewing.

### Always Be My Maybe

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 90% critics / 73% audience
- **Stars:** Randall Park, Ali Wong

Randall Park and Ali Wong star as childhood friends who reconnect as adults and navigate the complicated territory between friendship and romance. The movie is consistently funny, Wong and Park are a fantastic pairing, and there's a Keanu Reeves cameo that is genuinely one of the funniest things in any Netflix movie. Period.

### Look Both Ways

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 68% critics / 67% audience
- **Stars:** Lili Reinhart

Lili Reinhart stars in this romantic comedy that explores the classic "what if?" scenario. After a pregnancy test on the night of her college graduation, the film splits into two parallel timelines showing how her life unfolds differently based on the result. It's a clever concept executed with warmth and humor.

### Someone Great

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 83% critics / 78% audience
- **Stars:** Gina Rodriguez

Gina Rodriguez leads this breakup comedy about a music journalist who plans one last epic night out in New York City with her two best friends after a devastating split. It's funny, emotional, and captures the specific energy of your late twenties with remarkable accuracy. The friendship dynamics are the real heart of the movie.

## Dark Comedies and Satires

### Don't Look Up

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 56% critics / 78% audience
- **Stars:** Leonardo DiCaprio, Jennifer Lawrence, Meryl Streep

Adam McKay's star-studded satire about two astronomers trying to warn the world about an approaching comet is as relevant as ever. Your enjoyment will largely depend on your tolerance for satire that hits uncomfortably close to home. Love it or hate it, it's impossible to ignore.

### Glass Onion: A Knives Out Mystery

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 93% critics / 82% audience
- **Stars:** Daniel Craig, Edward Norton, Janelle Monáe

The sequel to *Knives Out* is arguably even funnier than the original. Daniel Craig returns as Benoit Blanc, this time investigating a murder mystery on a tech billionaire's private Greek island. The satire of Silicon Valley culture is razor-sharp, and the ensemble is clearly having a blast. It's smart, fun, and endlessly rewatchable.

### The Ballad of Buster Scruggs

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 91% critics / 74% audience
- **Directors:** Joel & Ethan Coen

The Coen Brothers' anthology Western is a darkly funny collection of six short stories set in the American frontier. Each segment has its own distinct tone, from broad slapstick to haunting tragedy, but they're united by the Coens' signature wit and impeccable craftsmanship. Tim Blake Nelson's opening segment as the singing cowboy Buster Scruggs is worth the price of admission alone.

## Action Comedies for When You Want Both

### Murder Mystery and Murder Mystery 2

- **Stars:** Adam Sandler, Jennifer Aniston
- **Great for:** Easy-watching comfort comedy

Adam Sandler and Jennifer Aniston team up for these lightweight but enjoyable comedies about a couple who keep getting entangled in murder investigations while on vacation. They're not going to win any awards, but they're the definition of easy-watching comfort comedy. Sometimes that's exactly what you need.

### The Adam Project

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 68% critics / 74% audience
- **Stars:** Ryan Reynolds, Mark Ruffalo, Jennifer Garner

Ryan Reynolds stars in this sci-fi action-comedy about a time-traveling pilot who teams up with his younger self to save the future. Reynolds does what Reynolds does best — delivering rapid-fire quips while navigating big action set pieces. Mark Ruffalo and Jennifer Garner add heart as his parents, and the movie strikes a surprisingly emotional chord beneath all the jokes.

### Hustle

- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 92% critics / 92% audience
- **Stars:** Adam Sandler, Queen Latifah

Okay, this one's more of a dramedy, but Adam Sandler's performance as a basketball scout who discovers an extraordinary talent in Spain is one of his best in years. It's funny, heartfelt, and features actual NBA players in supporting roles that add authenticity. If you've written off Sandler, this is the movie that will change your mind.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the funniest movies on Netflix right now?

As of 2026, top picks include *Nobody 2* for action-comedy, *Glass Onion* for smart mystery-comedy, *Superbad* for classic teen humor, and *Always Be My Maybe* for rom-com laughs. Netflix rotates its catalog frequently, but these titles have been reliably available throughout 2026.

### Does Netflix have good stand-up comedy specials?

Yes — Netflix is the largest platform for stand-up comedy specials. Beyond Jeff Ross's new 2026 special, the platform features hundreds of specials from comedians like Dave Chappelle, Ali Wong, John Mulaney, Hannah Gadsby, and many more. New specials drop almost weekly.

### What's the best comedy movie for a date night on Netflix?

*Set It Up* and *Always Be My Maybe* are ideal date night picks — they're romantic, funny, and feel-good without being too cheesy. For something more adventurous, *The Grand Budapest Hotel* offers visual beauty and wit. Avoid *Don't Look Up* for a first date (too divisive).

### Are there any new comedy movies coming to Netflix in 2026?

Netflix regularly adds new comedy films and stand-up specials throughout the year. Check the "New & Popular" section or Netflix's Tudum blog for upcoming releases. Major comedy releases typically drop on Fridays.

### What happened to some comedies that used to be on Netflix?

Netflix's licensing deals mean movies rotate in and out of the catalog. If a favorite comedy has left Netflix, it may return in the future or be available on another streaming platform. Use services like JustWatch to track where specific movies are currently streaming.
