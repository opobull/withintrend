Sometimes you just need a good laugh. Whether you've had a long day at work, you're hanging out with friends, or you just want something light and entertaining, a great comedy movie hits different. Netflix's comedy library in 2026 is stacked with options — from sharp-witted originals to beloved classics that never get old.

We've combed through Netflix's current catalog to bring you the best comedy movies streaming right now. This list covers everything from slapstick and rom-coms to dark comedies and action-comedies, so no matter what flavor of funny you're craving, we've got you covered.

## Best New Comedy Movies on Netflix (2026)

### Nobody 2

The sequel to the surprise hit action-comedy is now streaming on Netflix, and it delivers everything fans loved about the original — and then some. Bob Odenkirk returns as the unassuming family man with a very particular set of skills, and the action sequences are even more creative and brutal this time around. With a 76% critics score and 88% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes, it's the perfect blend of laughs and adrenaline. If you loved the first one, this is a no-brainer.

### Jeff Ross: Take a Banana for the Ride

The Roastmaster General himself delivers a hilarious new stand-up special that landed on Netflix in March 2026. Jeff Ross is at his sharpest here, roasting everyone from politicians to audience members with the kind of fearless comedy that's made him a legend. It's the perfect thing to throw on when you want quick, punchy laughs without committing to a full movie.

### Blue Moon

This charming comedy arrived on Netflix in late 2025 and has been steadily climbing the most-watched lists ever since. With an impressive 89% critics score and 76% audience score, *Blue Moon* offers a witty, character-driven story that balances humor with genuine heart. It's the kind of movie that surprises you — you go in expecting a light comedy and come out genuinely moved.

## Classic Comedies That Never Get Old

### The Grand Budapest Hotel

Wes Anderson's masterpiece is a visual feast wrapped in a hilarious caper. Ralph Fiennes delivers a career-best performance as the legendary concierge Gustave H., and the ensemble cast — including Tony Revolori, Saoirse Ronan, Jeff Goldblum, Willem Dafoe, and Edward Norton — is absolutely stacked. Every frame is a painting, every line is quotable, and it somehow manages to be both laugh-out-loud funny and deeply melancholic.

### Superbad

The 2007 teen comedy that defined a generation is still just as funny nearly two decades later. Jonah Hill and Michael Cera are perfectly cast as two high school seniors trying to buy alcohol for a party before graduation. The McLovin subplot alone is worth the rewatch. If you haven't seen it since it came out, revisit it — the jokes hold up remarkably well.

### The Nice Guys

Shane Black's criminally underrated buddy comedy set in 1970s Los Angeles pairs Ryan Gosling and Russell Crowe as a private eye and an enforcer investigating a missing girl. Gosling's physical comedy is absolutely brilliant, and his chemistry with Crowe is electric. This movie deserved to be a bigger hit, and its cult following continues to grow on Netflix.

### Knives Out

Rian Johnson's whodunit is as funny as it is clever. Daniel Craig's Benoit Blanc is an instant-classic detective character, and the ensemble cast — including Ana de Armas, Chris Evans, Jamie Lee Curtis, and Toni Collette — makes every scene a delight. The mystery is satisfying, but it's the sharp humor and social commentary that elevate it above standard genre fare.

## Best Rom-Coms on Netflix

### Set It Up

This 2018 Netflix original is a modern rom-com classic. Two overworked assistants (Zoey Deutch and Glen Powell) decide to set up their demanding bosses to make their own lives easier, only to develop feelings for each other in the process. It's charming, funny, and features genuinely great chemistry between its leads. If you love the genre, this is essential viewing.

### Always Be My Maybe

Randall Park and Ali Wong star as childhood friends who reconnect as adults and navigate the complicated territory between friendship and romance. The movie is consistently funny, Wong and Park are a fantastic pairing, and there's a Keanu Reeves cameo that is genuinely one of the funniest things in any Netflix movie. Period.

### Look Both Ways

Lili Reinhart stars in this romantic comedy that explores the classic "what if?" scenario. After a pregnancy test on the night of her college graduation, the film splits into two parallel timelines showing how her life unfolds differently based on the result. It's a clever concept executed with warmth and humor, and Reinhart's performance anchors the whole thing beautifully.

### Someone Great

Gina Rodriguez leads this breakup comedy about a music journalist who plans one last epic night out in New York City with her two best friends after a devastating split. It's funny, emotional, and captures the specific energy of your late twenties with remarkable accuracy. The friendship dynamics are the real heart of the movie.

## Dark Comedies and Satires

### Don't Look Up

Adam McKay's star-studded satire about two astronomers trying to warn the world about an approaching comet is as relevant as ever. Leonardo DiCaprio, Jennifer Lawrence, Meryl Streep, and Cate Blanchett lead a massive cast in a film that's simultaneously hilarious and deeply unsettling. Your enjoyment will largely depend on your tolerance for satire that hits uncomfortably close to home.

### Glass Onion: A Knives Out Mystery

The sequel to *Knives Out* is arguably even funnier than the original. Daniel Craig returns as Benoit Blanc, this time investigating a murder mystery on a tech billionaire's private Greek island. The satire of Silicon Valley culture is razor-sharp, and the ensemble — including Edward Norton, Janelle Monáe, and Dave Bautista — is clearly having a blast. It's smart, fun, and endlessly rewatchable.

### The Ballad of Buster Scruggs

The Coen Brothers' anthology Western is a darkly funny collection of six short stories set in the American frontier. Each segment has its own distinct tone, from broad slapstick to haunting tragedy, but they're united by the Coens' signature wit and impeccable craftsmanship. Tim Blake Nelson's opening segment as the singing cowboy Buster Scruggs is worth the price of admission alone.

## Action Comedies for When You Want Both

### Murder Mystery and Murder Mystery 2

Adam Sandler and Jennifer Aniston team up for these lightweight but enjoyable comedies about a couple who keep getting entangled in murder investigations while on vacation. They're not going to win any awards, but they're the definition of easy-watching comfort comedy. Sometimes that's exactly what you need.

### The Adam Project

Ryan Reynolds stars in this sci-fi action-comedy about a time-traveling pilot who teams up with his younger self to save the future. Reynolds does what Reynolds does best — delivering rapid-fire quips while navigating big action set pieces. Mark Ruffalo and Jennifer Garner add heart as his parents, and the movie strikes a surprisingly emotional chord beneath all the jokes.

### Hustle

Okay, this one's more of a dramedy, but Adam Sandler's performance as a basketball scout who discovers an extraordinary talent in Spain is one of his best in years. It's funny, heartfelt, and features actual NBA players in supporting roles that add authenticity. If you've written off Sandler, this is the movie that will change your mind.

## How to Pick Your Perfect Comedy Night

- **Want guaranteed laughs?** *Superbad*, *The Nice Guys*, or *Always Be My Maybe*
- **In the mood for romance?** *Set It Up* or *Look Both Ways*
- **Feeling intellectual?** *The Grand Budapest Hotel* or *Glass Onion*
- **Want action with your comedy?** *Nobody 2* or *The Adam Project*
- **Need something dark?** *Don't Look Up* or *The Ballad of Buster Scruggs*

Netflix's comedy library is deep enough that you could watch a different comedy every night for months. The platform continues to invest in comedy originals while maintaining a solid rotation of licensed titles, ensuring there's always something fresh to discover. So grab your snacks, settle in, and let the laughs begin.
