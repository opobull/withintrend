## Celebrity Book Clubs Are Bigger Than Ever

Celebrity book clubs have evolved from casual endorsements into full-blown cultural forces that can launch unknown authors onto bestseller lists overnight. In 2026, the most influential celebrity book clubs command audiences of millions and have become the primary way many Americans discover their next read. The power these clubs wield in the publishing industry is staggering — a single pick can mean hundreds of thousands of additional sales and often leads directly to film or television adaptations.

But not all celebrity book clubs are created equal. Some consistently deliver outstanding picks that challenge and delight readers, while others feel more like marketing vehicles than genuine literary communities. Here is a breakdown of which clubs deserve your attention and which you can safely skip.

## The Top Tier

### Reese's Book Club (Reese Witherspoon)

Reese Witherspoon's book club remains the gold standard in 2026, and for good reason. Since its launch, the club has maintained a remarkably consistent track record of selecting books that are both commercially appealing and genuinely excellent. The focus leans toward fiction with strong female protagonists, but the range within that framework is impressive — from literary fiction to thrillers to memoirs.

What sets Reese's club apart is the pipeline from page to screen. Witherspoon's production company, Hello Sunshine, has adapted numerous book club picks into films and series, creating an ecosystem where a book selection can become the next major streaming hit. For readers, this adds an exciting layer — you might be reading the source material for next year's buzziest show.

The community engagement is also strong. Monthly discussions happen across social media platforms, and the club's Instagram account regularly features reader reactions and author interactions. It feels less like a celebrity endorsement and more like a genuine community of readers.

### Oprah's Book Club

The original celebrity book club continues to deliver quality picks decades after its founding. Oprah's selections tend to be more literary and thematically weighty than some competitors, frequently tackling subjects like race, identity, trauma, and social justice. The books are often challenging but always rewarding, and Oprah's deep-dive author interviews provide context and insight that enhance the reading experience.

The club's partnership with Apple TV Plus has added a multimedia dimension, with filmed author conversations and curated reading guides available to subscribers. This combination of literary ambition and production quality keeps Oprah's club relevant and influential even as newer competitors emerge.

### Read With Jenna (Jenna Bush Hager)

Jenna Bush Hager's Today Show book club has quietly become one of the most reliable sources for book recommendations. The picks span a wide range of genres and styles, from debut novels to established authors, with a particular strength in discovering diverse voices that might otherwise be overlooked. Hager's genuine enthusiasm during her book segments is infectious, and her picks rarely disappoint.

The community aspect is more traditional than some clubs, centered around the Today Show audience and social media discussions, but the quality of selections more than compensates. If you only follow one book club, this is a strong contender.

## The Solid Middle Ground

### Good Morning America Book Club

GMA's club picks tend toward crowd-pleasers — commercially appealing books that are accessible and entertaining without necessarily pushing literary boundaries. This is not a criticism; sometimes you want a page-turner that keeps you up past midnight, and GMA delivers those consistently.

The club benefits from the massive GMA audience and regular author appearances on the show. The picks are generally safe choices that most readers will enjoy, making it a good option if you want reliable entertainment without much risk of encountering a book that is not to your taste.

### Dua Lipa's Service95 Book Club

An unexpected entrant that has earned genuine literary credibility, Dua Lipa's book club reflects her eclectic interests with picks ranging from contemporary fiction to nonfiction, philosophy, and poetry. The selections tend to be more internationally focused than most celebrity clubs, introducing English-language readers to translated works they might never encounter otherwise.

This club is worth following if you want to expand your reading horizons beyond the typical American and British literary mainstream. The picks are often surprising, sometimes challenging, and consistently interesting.

## How Celebrity Book Clubs Shape Publishing

The influence of these clubs on the publishing industry cannot be overstated. A celebrity book club pick can increase a book's sales by 500 to 1,000 percent within weeks. For debut authors, a selection can mean the difference between obscurity and a full-time writing career. Publishers actively pitch books to celebrity clubs, and securing a spot has become a major strategic goal for marketing departments.

This influence has both positive and negative aspects. On the positive side, celebrity book clubs have measurably increased reading rates, particularly among demographics that were not previously regular book buyers. They have also amplified diverse voices and brought international literature to wider audiences.

The concerns are about homogenization. When a handful of celebrities effectively curate reading lists for millions of people, there is a risk that certain types of books get outsized attention while equally worthy titles are ignored. The books that get selected tend to share certain characteristics — accessibility, film adaptation potential, topic relevance — that may not capture the full breadth of what contemporary literature offers.

## How to Get the Most Out of Book Clubs

Joining a celebrity book club is free and requires nothing more than following their social media accounts or signing up for newsletters. The real value comes from active participation in discussions, which forces you to think more critically about what you read.

Consider following two or three clubs with different sensibilities to ensure variety in your reading diet. Use the picks as a starting point, not a final destination — if a book club pick introduces you to a new author, explore their backlist. If a pick covers a topic that interests you, seek out the nonfiction sources on the same subject.

And remember, it is perfectly fine to disagree with a pick. Not every book will resonate with every reader, and a book club that never introduces you to something outside your comfort zone is not challenging you enough. The best book clubs — celebrity or otherwise — push you to read things you would not have chosen on your own.
