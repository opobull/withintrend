## Biggest New Shows on Max in 2026

### A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms

- **Premiere:** 2026
- **Based on:** George R.R. Martin's *Tales of Dunk and Egg* novellas
- **Genre:** Fantasy adventure

The Game of Thrones universe expands once again with this adaptation of George R.R. Martin's *Tales of Dunk and Egg* novellas. Set roughly a century before the events of *Game of Thrones*, the series follows the unlikely partnership between a naïve but courageous hedge knight named Ser Duncan the Tall and his cunning squire, Egg — who happens to be a Targaryen prince in disguise. The show trades the epic battles and political machinations of its predecessor for a more intimate, character-driven adventure story that still feels unmistakably like Westeros. Early reviews have been overwhelmingly positive.

**Why it matters:** This is the first Game of Thrones spinoff that focuses on smaller-scale storytelling rather than epic spectacle, proving the universe has room for diverse types of stories.

### The Pitt

- **Stars:** Noah Wyle
- **Format:** Real-time (each episode = 1 hour of a 15-hour shift)
- **Genre:** Medical drama

This medical drama starring Noah Wyle has become one of Max's breakout hits of early 2026. Set in a Pittsburgh trauma center, each episode covers one hour in real-time during a 15-hour shift. The format creates relentless tension and gives the show a uniquely immersive feel. If you've been missing *ER*-style medical drama, *The Pitt* delivers exactly what you've been craving — but with a modern edge.

### The Dark Wizard

- **Premiere:** April 14, 2026
- **Network:** HBO Original
- **Status:** Upcoming

One of the most anticipated Max originals coming in April 2026, *The Dark Wizard* is the latest HBO Original that promises to continue the platform's tradition of bold, cinematic storytelling. Details are being kept under wraps, but the HBO Original label alone puts it on the must-watch radar.

### Euphoria Season 3

- **Stars:** Zendaya
- **Genre:** Teen drama
- **Seasons available:** 3

The long-awaited return of Zendaya and the *Euphoria* cast is one of the biggest TV events of 2026. After years of delays and behind-the-scenes drama, Season 3 promises to push boundaries even further with its visually stunning cinematography and emotionally devastating storylines. Love it or hate it, *Euphoria* remains one of the most visually ambitious shows on television.

### DTF St.

One of Max's new comedy entries, *DTF St.* has been generating buzz for its sharp writing and ensemble cast. The series brings a fresh comedic voice to the platform, which has historically leaned heavily on drama.

## Essential HBO Classics on Max

### The Sopranos

- **IMDb:** 9.2/10 | **RT:** 92% critics
- **Episodes:** 86 across 6 seasons
- **Why watch:** The show that launched the golden age of television

James Gandolfini's Tony Soprano remains one of the most complex characters in TV history — a mob boss struggling with panic attacks who starts seeing a therapist. *The Sopranos* is funny, violent, deeply psychological, and endlessly rewatchable. If you've never seen it, you're in for a treat. If you have, it's worth revisiting.

### The Wire

- **IMDb:** 9.3/10 | **RT:** 94% critics
- **Episodes:** 60 across 5 seasons
- **Often called:** The greatest TV show ever made

David Simon's sprawling examination of Baltimore's institutions — the drug trade, the docks, city hall, the schools, and the media — demands patience (the first few episodes are deliberately slow), but the payoff is enormous. Each season tackles a different facet of urban America, building into a unified portrait of a city and a country struggling with systemic failure.

### The Leftovers

- **IMDb:** 8.3/10 | **RT:** 93% critics
- **Episodes:** 28 across 3 seasons
- **Created by:** Damon Lindelof and Tom Perrotta

Widely considered one of the greatest television shows ever made, *The Leftovers* explores what happens in the aftermath of a Rapture-like event where 2% of the world's population suddenly vanishes. Justin Theroux and Carrie Coon deliver career-defining performances. It's a profound meditation on grief, faith, and the human need for meaning. This is something truly special.

### Succession

- **IMDb:** 8.8/10 | **RT:** 95% critics
- **Episodes:** 39 across 4 seasons
- **Emmy Awards:** 19 wins

The Roy family saga is a masterclass in dark comedy and family dysfunction. Following the power struggle within a global media empire, *Succession* features some of the sharpest writing and most quotable dialogue in recent memory. Jeremy Strong, Sarah Snook, and Brian Cox are uniformly excellent. The finale remains one of the most debated endings in TV history.

### Band of Brothers

- **IMDb:** 9.4/10 | **RT:** 97% critics
- **Episodes:** 10
- **Producers:** Steven Spielberg and Tom Hanks

Steven Spielberg and Tom Hanks' epic miniseries about Easy Company during World War II remains the gold standard for war television. Based on Stephen Ambrose's book, it's 10 episodes of extraordinary television that will leave you emotionally wrecked in the best possible way.

### Barry

Hader's performance is phenomenal.

### Hacks

- **IMDb:** 8.2/10 | **RT:** 98% critics
- **Stars:** Jean Smart (Emmy winner for this role)

Jean Smart delivers an Emmy-winning performance as Deborah Vance, a legendary Las Vegas comedian forced to work with a young comedy writer. *Hacks* is sharp, funny, and surprisingly touching. It's one of the best comedies on any platform right now.

### The White Lotus

- **RT:** 89% critics
- **Format:** Anthology (new cast/location each season)
- **Created by:** Mike White

Mike White's satirical anthology series examining wealth, privilege, and human behavior at luxury resorts became a genuine cultural phenomenon. Each season features a new cast and location, but the sharp social commentary and darkly funny tone remain consistent.

### Station Eleven

- **IMDb:** 7.8/10 | **RT:** 98% critics
- **Episodes:** 10 (limited series)
- **Based on:** Emily St. John Mandel's novel

This post-apocalyptic miniseries is quietly one of the best things Max has ever produced. Following interconnected characters in the years after a devastating pandemic, *Station Eleven* is hopeful, beautiful, and profoundly moving. It's not about survival — it's about what makes survival worthwhile.

### Somebody Somewhere

- **RT:** 98% critics
- **Stars:** Bridget Everett

Bridget Everett stars in this understated comedy-drama about a woman finding her place in her small Kansas hometown. It's warm, funny, and deeply human — a show that celebrates the weird, wonderful people who don't quite fit in anywhere.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Max the same as HBO Max?

Yes — HBO Max was rebranded to simply "Max" in 2023. The service still includes all HBO content plus additional content from Warner Bros. Discovery brands. All existing HBO Max subscriptions were automatically converted to Max.

### What's the best show to start with on Max?

It depends on your taste. For prestige drama, start with *The Sopranos* or *Succession*. For something newer, *A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms* or *The Pitt* are excellent entry points. For comedy, *Barry* or *Hacks* are instant favorites. For a limited series commitment, *Band of Brothers* or *Station Eleven* are perfect.

### How much does Max cost in 2026?

Max offers multiple tiers: **With Ads** (~$9.99/month), **Ad-Free** (~$15.99/month), and **Ultimate** (~$19.99/month, 4K + extra streams). Prices vary by region and may change, so check Max's website for current pricing.

### Is The Wire really worth watching?

Absolutely. *The Wire* demands patience — the first 3-4 episodes are deliberately slow as the show establishes its world and characters. But by mid-Season 1, you'll be completely hooked. It consistently ranks in critics' lists of the greatest TV shows ever made, and it only gets better with each season.

### Does Max have live sports?

Max has been expanding its sports offerings, particularly through partnerships with Turner Sports. NCAA basketball (including March Madness), NHL hockey, and select MLB games are available on the platform. For comprehensive sports coverage, you may want to pair Max with a dedicated sports service like Peacock or ESPN+.

### Can I download shows on Max for offline viewing?

Yes — the Ad-Free and Ultimate plans allow you to download select titles for offline viewing. The With Ads plan does not include download capability.
