## Introduction

Kohl's is one of the largest department store chains in the United States, operating over 1,100 stores nationwide. The company employs thousands of part-time and full-time retail associates and is known for its coupon-heavy shopping experience and Kohl's Cash rewards program. But what do the people actually working the floor think about the job?

This guide is based on a comprehensive review of dozens of real employee experiences shared across job review sites, forums, and social media — not a single person's opinion, but a balanced summary of what actual workers report.

Here's what you should know about working as a Kohl's associate in 2025–2026.

## What You'll Actually Do

Kohl's associates wear many hats, and your day-to-day tasks will depend on which department or area you're assigned to. The main areas include the sales floor, the customer service desk, the fitting rooms, and the registers.

On the sales floor, you'll organize merchandise, straighten racks, refold clothing, and restock products from the backroom. Kohl's stores are large — often spanning over 80,000 square feet — so maintaining a presentable store is a constant effort. Associates assigned to specific departments (such as home, women's, men's, or shoes) are expected to develop familiarity with their section's products and layout.

Many associates also spend time processing online orders for in-store pickup (BOPIS) and curbside delivery, which has become an increasingly important part of the role. During holiday seasons, the volume of online orders processed in-store can be significant.

Customer service interactions include helping shoppers find products, explaining Kohl's Cash and coupon policies (which can be confusing even for seasoned customers), and assisting with returns and exchanges.

## Pay & Hours

Kohl's associate pay typically ranges from **$11 to $15 per hour**, depending on location and experience. PayScale reports an average of approximately $12.12 per hour for sales associates, while Glassdoor estimates are slightly higher at around $15 per hour when factoring in all compensation data.

One notable benefit is that Kohl's pays **weekly**, which many associates appreciate for budgeting purposes. However, the base hourly rate is generally considered low compared to competitors like Target, which has maintained a $15+ minimum wage.

Part-time associates usually receive between 12 and 25 hours per week, with significant variation depending on the season. Holiday hours (October through January) can be generous, but January through March often brings a sharp reduction. Scheduling can be inconsistent, and some employees report irregular shift patterns that make it difficult to plan their week.

The employee discount is generally **15% off** regular-priced merchandise, which can be combined with store coupons for additional savings. Full-time employees have access to health insurance, 401(k) plans, and paid time off, but these benefits are not available to most part-time workers.

## Pros

1. **Weekly pay** — Getting paid every week rather than biweekly is a practical benefit that many associates value for managing their finances.

2. **Friendly team culture** — Many reviews emphasize the positive coworker relationships at Kohl's. The team atmosphere is frequently described as friendly and supportive.

3. **Employee discount plus coupons** — The 15% employee discount stacks with Kohl's frequent coupons, allowing associates to get solid deals on clothing and home goods.

4. **Flexible scheduling for students** — Kohl's is generally accommodating with school schedules and allows associates to specify their availability.

5. **Seasonal hiring opportunities** — Kohl's hires large numbers of seasonal employees, making it accessible for people looking for temporary holiday work that could lead to permanent positions.

## Cons

1. **Low pay compared to competitors** — This is the most frequent criticism. Many associates feel Kohl's pays below what similar retailers offer, particularly compared to Target and Costco.

2. **Kohl's Charge pressure** — Associates are expected to sign customers up for the Kohl's Charge credit card, and this metric is actively tracked by management. Many workers find this requirement frustrating.

3. **Inconsistent scheduling** — Random or unpredictable shift assignments are a common complaint. Some employees report managers scheduling them outside their stated availability or changing shifts with little notice.

4. **Limited upward mobility** — Several long-tenured employees note that advancement opportunities are scarce, particularly as the company has reduced management positions in recent years.

5. **Physically demanding** — The large store footprint means a lot of walking, and associates in certain departments (shoes, home) do significant lifting and organizing throughout their shifts.

## Tips for New Employees

1. **Understand the coupon and Kohl's Cash system** — Customers will constantly ask questions about how coupons stack, when Kohl's Cash can be redeemed, and what exclusions apply. Being knowledgeable here will reduce friction at the register and on the floor.

2. **Be prepared for the credit card push** — Develop a quick, low-pressure pitch for the Kohl's Charge card. Management will track your numbers, but a natural approach works better than an aggressive one.

3. **Wear comfortable shoes** — The stores are large and you'll walk miles during a single shift. Good footwear is essential.

4. **Volunteer for BOPIS and online order fulfillment** — These tasks are increasingly important to Kohl's operations and showing proficiency can lead to more hours and favorable scheduling.

5. **Take advantage of associate shopping events** — Kohl's periodically runs enhanced discount events for employees. Combine these with coupons for significant savings.

## FAQ

**Does Kohl's pay weekly or biweekly?**
Kohl's pays weekly, which is a frequently noted benefit among employees. Payday is typically on Friday.

**How much does a Kohl's associate make per hour?**
Most part-time associates earn between $11 and $15 per hour depending on location. The national average for sales associates is approximately $12–$13 per hour.

**Is Kohl's a good seasonal job?**
Yes. Kohl's hires large numbers of seasonal employees during the holidays, the training is quick, and some seasonal workers are offered permanent positions after the season ends.

## Conclusion

Working at Kohl's as an associate offers weekly pay, a friendly team environment, and a stackable employee discount that delivers real value. The scheduling flexibility makes it a practical choice for students and those balancing multiple commitments. However, the below-average pay, credit card sales pressure, and inconsistent scheduling are significant drawbacks that many employees find frustrating over time. Kohl's is a reasonable option for part-time retail work, particularly during the holiday season or as a first job, but those seeking competitive pay may want to explore alternatives.
