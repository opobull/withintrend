## Introduction

Panda Express is the largest American Chinese restaurant chain, with over 2,400 locations serving wok-prepared dishes to millions of customers every week. Behind the serving line, kitchen team members are responsible for preparing the entrees and sides that make Panda Express a go-to for quick Asian-inspired meals. It is a role that blends real cooking skills with the speed and demands of fast food.

This guide is based on a comprehensive review of dozens of real employee experiences shared across job review sites, forums, and social media — not a single person's opinion, but a balanced summary of what actual workers report.

If you are considering a kitchen position at Panda Express, here is the full breakdown.

## What You'll Actually Do

As a Panda Express kitchen team member, your primary job is preparing the restaurant's entrees and side dishes using wok cooking techniques. A typical shift involves prepping ingredients (chopping vegetables, portioning proteins), cooking dishes in large woks, maintaining the quality and temperature of food on the serving line, and keeping the kitchen clean and organized.

Unlike many fast food kitchens that rely primarily on fryers and microwaves, Panda Express requires genuine wok cooking. New kitchen team members often start with simpler tasks like preparing sides (fried rice, chow mein, mixed vegetables) before progressing to entrees like Orange Chicken, Beijing Beef, and Kung Pao Chicken. The transition to entree cooking is a significant step that requires learning specific recipes, techniques, and timing.

Kitchen cleanliness is taken seriously. Workers must clean woks between batches, maintain sanitation standards, manage food storage, and handle end-of-shift deep cleaning. Closing shifts involve scrubbing woks, cleaning all surfaces, mopping floors, and taking out trash — a process that can take an hour or more after the store closes.

## Pay & Hours

Panda Express kitchen team members typically earn between **$13 and $18 per hour**, which is above average for the fast food industry. In higher cost-of-living areas, starting pay can reach $19 or more. Panda Express is generally recognized as paying better than most fast food competitors, which is a frequently cited reason employees choose to work there.

Part-time kitchen workers usually log **20 to 32 hours per week**, though hours can fluctuate based on store traffic and staffing levels. A common frustration is that management has been pushing to cut hours while increasing the amount of work expected during shorter shifts. Full-time positions with 35 to 40 hours are available, especially for reliable workers who demonstrate cooking ability.

Panda Express offers some benefits even to part-time workers, including access to the company's Panda Care program and educational assistance opportunities.

## Pros

1. **Above-average pay for fast food**: Panda Express consistently pays more than many competitors, making it an attractive option for kitchen workers.
2. **Learn real cooking skills**: Wok cooking is a genuine culinary technique. Workers leave with skills that are more transferable than typical fast food experience.
3. **Free meals during shifts**: Most locations provide a complimentary meal per shift, which employees appreciate.
4. **Company invests in development**: Panda Express offers educational programs and emphasizes personal growth, going beyond what most fast food chains provide.
5. **No prior experience required**: The company hires workers with zero kitchen experience and trains them on the job.

## Cons

1. **Physically demanding and exhausting**: Wok cooking involves standing for hours, lifting heavy pans, and working in high heat — the physical toll is significant.
2. **Hours being cut while expectations increase**: Many employees report that management is reducing shift lengths while expecting the same or more output.
3. **High turnover leads to understaffing**: Constant employee turnover means kitchen workers frequently need to cover for missing team members.
4. **High standards create pressure**: Panda Express enforces strict food quality and cleanliness standards, which some workers find overwhelming, especially during busy periods.
5. **Closing shifts are labor-intensive**: The deep cleaning required after closing is extensive and adds significant time to the end of a shift.

## Tips for New Employees

1. **Master the sides first**: Fried rice and chow mein are where you will start. Getting these right quickly will build your confidence and earn trust from the kitchen lead.
2. **Communicate with your managers**: Panda Express values employees who communicate openly. Asking questions and showing interest in learning new dishes will accelerate your development.
3. **Wear comfortable, heat-resistant clothing**: The kitchen is hot. Breathable fabrics and supportive shoes make a significant difference over a long shift.
4. **Learn the company's values**: Panda Express emphasizes personal development and a positive attitude. Understanding and embodying these values will help you succeed and advance.

## FAQ

**Is Panda Express kitchen harder than other fast food cooking positions?**
Many employees say yes. The wok cooking technique is more complex than typical fast food preparation, and the quality standards are higher. However, workers also note that the skills gained are more valuable and transferable than what you learn at most chains.

**Can kitchen team members become chefs at Panda Express?**
Yes. Kitchen team members can advance to cook, then chef, and eventually to kitchen manager or assistant manager roles. Panda Express promotes heavily from within, and ambitious workers can move up quickly.

**Does Panda Express provide training for kitchen workers?**
Yes. New kitchen team members receive hands-on training that covers food prep, wok cooking techniques, sanitation standards, and kitchen procedures. The training period typically lasts one to two weeks, though fully mastering all entrees takes longer.

## Conclusion

Working in the kitchen at Panda Express is one of the more rewarding fast food positions available, particularly for workers interested in developing real cooking skills. The pay is above industry average, the training is substantive, and the company's emphasis on personal development sets it apart from most competitors. However, the physical demands are significant, hours may be inconsistent, and the high standards can feel intense during busy shifts. Panda Express kitchen work is best suited for workers who enjoy cooking, want to build skills beyond basic fast food preparation, and are looking for an employer that invests in their growth. If you are willing to put in the physical effort and embrace the learning curve, the kitchen at Panda Express offers a fast food experience that actually teaches you something valuable.
