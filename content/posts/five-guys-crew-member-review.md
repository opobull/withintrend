## Introduction

Five Guys has built a reputation for its fresh, made-to-order burgers and generous fries — but what is it actually like to work behind the counter? With thousands of locations across the United States and a fiercely loyal customer base, Five Guys is one of the most popular fast food employers for part-time and entry-level workers. Whether you are a teenager looking for a first job or someone seeking flexible hours, Five Guys crew member positions are widely available and frequently hiring.

This guide is based on a comprehensive review of dozens of real employee experiences shared across job review sites, forums, and social media — not a single person's opinion, but a balanced summary of what actual workers report.

If you are considering applying to Five Guys, here is everything you need to know about the job before you clock in.

## What You'll Actually Do

As a Five Guys crew member, your responsibilities cover nearly every aspect of restaurant operations. Unlike some fast food chains that assign workers to fixed stations, Five Guys crew members are expected to rotate through multiple positions throughout a shift.

A typical day involves taking customer orders at the register, preparing burgers and hot dogs on the grill, operating the fry station, restocking ingredients, and keeping the restaurant clean. Five Guys is known for its "secret shopper" program, where mystery shoppers evaluate the store on cleanliness, food quality, and customer service. This means there is a strong emphasis on following procedures precisely — from the number of scoops of fries to how toppings are arranged.

Closing shifts are particularly demanding. Workers report spending significant time scrubbing grills, mopping floors, wiping down every surface, and taking out trash. The cleaning standards at Five Guys are notably higher than many competing fast food chains, and employees are expected to leave the store spotless before heading home.

## Pay & Hours

Five Guys crew members typically earn between **$12 and $17 per hour**, depending on location and local minimum wage laws. In higher cost-of-living areas, starting pay can reach $18 or more. Many employees note that Five Guys pays above average compared to other fast food chains, which is frequently cited as one of the top reasons people choose to work there.

One notable perk is the **secret shopper bonus**. When a store scores well on a mystery shopper visit, crew members on that shift can receive a bonus, sometimes ranging from $1 to $3 extra per hour for the pay period. However, a poor score can mean no bonus, which some workers find stressful.

Part-time crew members generally work **15 to 30 hours per week**, though getting consistent hours can be a challenge at some locations. Full-time positions are available but may be harder to secure as a new hire. Shifts typically range from 4 to 8 hours, with lunch and dinner rushes being the busiest periods.

## Pros

1. **Above-average pay for fast food**: Five Guys consistently pays more than many competitors, and the secret shopper bonus adds extra earning potential.
2. **Free meals during shifts**: Crew members typically receive a free meal each shift, which is a solid perk for anyone watching their budget.
3. **Fast-paced, time-flies environment**: Many workers say the busy nature of Five Guys means shifts go by quickly.
4. **Simple menu makes training manageable**: With a relatively small menu focused on burgers, fries, and hot dogs, learning the ropes is straightforward.
5. **Good first job experience**: The structured environment teaches discipline, teamwork, and customer service skills that transfer well to future positions.

## Cons

1. **Intense cleaning expectations**: The level of cleaning required, especially on closing shifts, goes far beyond what most fast food jobs demand.
2. **Secret shopper pressure**: While the bonus is nice, the constant evaluation creates stress and anxiety for many crew members.
3. **Management quality varies greatly**: Multiple reviews highlight that poor management is a common issue, with some shift leaders described as unsupportive or disengaged.
4. **Physically demanding work**: Standing for entire shifts, lifting heavy boxes of potatoes, and working near hot grills takes a toll on the body.
5. **Inconsistent scheduling**: Part-time workers frequently report difficulty getting enough hours, especially during slower seasons.

## Tips for New Employees

1. **Learn the secret shopper criteria early**: Understanding what mystery shoppers evaluate will help you earn bonuses and avoid negative attention from management.
2. **Get comfortable with the fry station**: The fry station is often considered the hardest position, so volunteering to learn it early will make you a more valuable team member.
3. **Wear comfortable, slip-resistant shoes**: You will be on your feet the entire shift on floors that can get greasy. Investing in good shoes makes a huge difference.
4. **Be prepared for closing shifts**: If you are scheduled to close, expect to stay 30 to 60 minutes after the restaurant officially closes to finish cleaning.
5. **Communicate with your manager about hours**: If you need more shifts, make it known early and show reliability. Managers tend to give more hours to dependable workers.

## FAQ

**Is Five Guys a good first job for teenagers?**
Many former employees say Five Guys is an excellent first job. The structured environment, teamwork focus, and above-average pay make it a strong starting point. However, it is more physically demanding and has higher cleaning standards than some other fast food options, so it is not the easiest entry-level position out there.

**Do Five Guys crew members get tips?**
Five Guys does not have a traditional tipping system. However, some locations have a tip jar at the counter, and crew members may occasionally receive small tips from customers. The main performance-based compensation comes from the secret shopper bonus program.

**How long does it take to get trained at Five Guys?**
Most crew members report that basic training takes about one to two weeks. During this time, you will rotate through the major stations — register, grill, fries, and cleaning. While the menu is simple, mastering the speed and quality standards takes a few more weeks of practice.

## Conclusion

Working at Five Guys as a crew member is a solid choice for anyone looking for a fast food job that pays above the industry average and offers a structured work environment. The secret shopper bonus program provides an opportunity to earn extra money, and the relatively simple menu makes the learning curve manageable. However, the job comes with higher-than-average cleaning expectations, inconsistent hours for part-timers, and management quality that can vary widely from store to store. Five Guys is best suited for workers who thrive in fast-paced environments, do not mind physical labor, and appreciate a workplace that holds everyone to a high standard. If you are willing to put in the effort and can handle the pressure of being evaluated by mystery shoppers, Five Guys can be a rewarding part-time gig.
